Mechanisms Slide 1




The SN2 mechanism is a bimolecular nucleophilic substitution reaction. Using the example above, the nitrile is the nucleophile which is attacking the methyl iodine (electrophile). From the crude scheme, the nitrile is coming in from the back displacing the iodine to give the product. Hence, this is why the SN2 reaction is known as the backside attack. Everything in this reaction happens at the same time, bonds forming and bonds breaking. Chemist call these type of reactions a concerted process, which means the all SN2 reactions are concerted reactions.

The reason the reaction is considered a bimolecular reaction is that two molecules are interacting with one another in the rate determining step to make the product. Since this is happening all at once, there are no intermediates in the SN2 mechanism.
